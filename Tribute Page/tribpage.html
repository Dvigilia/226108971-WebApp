Dr. Marie Curie

The first woman to win a Nobel Prize.

Here's a time line of Marie Curie:

  • 1867 - Born in Warsaw, Kingdom of Poland
  • 1891 - Followed her sister elder sister Bronislawa to study in Paris.
  • 1894 - Marie meets Pierre Curie, a fellow scientist, and they begin their collaboration and relationship.
  • 1895 - Marie and Pierre get married.
  • 1896 - Marie Curie receives her degree in physics, becoming the first woman in France to earn a degree in the field.
  • 1898 - Together with her husband Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel, Marie discovers the phenomenon of radioactivity, leading to her doctoral thesis on the subject.
  • 1903 - Marie Curie becomes the first woman to win a Nobel Prize, awarded jointly with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel, for their work on radioactivity.
  • 1906 - Pierre Curie tragically dies in a street accident.
  • 1910 - Marie Curie isolates pure radium metal.
  • 1911 - Marie Curie is awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for her discovery of radium and polonium.
  • 1914-1918 - During World War I, Marie Curie develops mobile radiography units to provide X-ray services to field hospitals.
  • 1921 - Marie Curie establishes the Radium Institute in Paris.
  • 1934 - Marie Curie dies on July 4 from aplastic anemia, likely caused by her prolonged exposure to radiation during her scientific research.
